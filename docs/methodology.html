Lynch & Graham Screener
Dashboard Methodology

Methodology

This screener runs two parallel valuation frameworks — Peter Lynch and Benjamin Graham — across the S&P 500, Dow 30, and Nasdaq-100 universe. Each framework outputs a fair value estimate and a buy/hold/avoid signal. A blended score combines both for easy sorting.

Peter Lynch Framework

Lynch's core idea is that a stock is fairly valued when its P/E ratio equals its earnings growth rate (PEG = 1). Adding the dividend yield gives the PEGY ratio for income-paying stocks. The screener uses the G+D method (growth plus dividends) as the primary Lynch signal and the PEG band method as a secondary signal.

Lynch Status

Column: Lynch_Lynch_Status — primary Lynch buy/hold/avoid signal based on the G+D fair value.

Strong Buy
Price is at or below 70% of Lynch fair value (EPS × (Growth% + Dividend%))
Buy
Price is at or below 100% of Lynch fair value
Hold
Price is at or below 130% of Lynch fair value
Avoid
Price is above 130% of Lynch fair value

PEG Band

Column: Lynch_Lynch_PEG_Band — secondary Lynch signal based on the conservative PEG fair value.

Strong Buy
Price is at or below 70% of conservative PEG fair value
Buy
Price is at or below the conservative PEG fair value (EPS × 0.8 × Growth%)
Hold
Price is at or below the standard PEG fair value (EPS × Growth%)
Avoid
Price is above the standard PEG fair value

PEG Status / PEGY Status

Columns: Lynch_PEG_Status and Lynch_PEGY_Status — classify the raw PEG and PEGY ratios.

Cheap
PEG (or PEGY) below 1.0 — stock trades below its growth-adjusted fair value
Reasonable
PEG (or PEGY) between 1.0 and 1.5 — fairly priced relative to growth
Rich
PEG (or PEGY) above 1.5 — stock trades at a premium to its growth rate

Company Categories

Column: Lynch_Lynch_Category — automatically assigned based on the EPS growth rate.

Slow Grower
Growth below 5% — mature or utility-type companies
Stalwart
Growth between 5% and 12% — large, steady compounders
Fast Grower
Growth above 12% — higher growth, but with more valuation risk

Key Metrics

Lynch_PEG
P/E ratio divided by Growth%. Below 1.0 = cheap; above 1.0 = rich.
Lynch_PEGY
P/E ratio divided by (Growth% + Dividend%). Rewards income-paying stocks.
Lynch_Lynch_Score
(Growth% + Dividend%) divided by P/E. Inverse of PEGY — higher is better.
Lynch_FV_GplusD
G+D fair value: EPS × (Growth% + Dividend%). The primary Lynch fair value estimate.
Lynch_Lynch_BuyPrice
Category-adjusted buy price — applies an extra discount for Fast Growers, less for Stalwarts.
Lynch_Lynch_Discount_Pct
How far below Lynch_BuyPrice the stock is currently trading. Positive = trading below buy price (good).

Discount Factors by Category

The buy price applies a category-specific discount to the conservative fair value:

Slow Grower
Buy at 75% of conservative fair value
Stalwart
Buy at 80% of conservative fair value
Fast Grower
Buy at 70% of conservative fair value (additional discount for growth risk)

Benjamin Graham Framework

Graham's core idea is to buy at a significant discount to intrinsic value — the "margin of safety." His formula adjusts fair value for the prevailing interest rate environment: higher rates reduce intrinsic value, lower rates increase it. The screener computes two Graham formula variants and uses the more conservative of the two as the fair value estimate.

Graham Status

Column: Graham_Graham_Status — classifies current price relative to Graham fair value.

Deep Buy
Price is at or below 60% of Graham fair value (40%+ margin of safety)
Buy
Price is at or below 80% of Graham fair value (20%+ margin of safety)
Watch
Price is at or below 95% of Graham fair value
Avoid
Price is above 95% of Graham fair value

Fair Value Formulas

Version A (Graham_Graham_VA): EPS × (8.5 + 2 × Growth%) × 4.4 ÷ AAA_Yield

8.5
Graham's no-growth P/E baseline — the P/E appropriate for a company with zero growth
2 × Growth%
Each percentage point of annual growth adds 2 points to the fair P/E
4.4
Historical AAA corporate bond yield when Graham wrote the formula (~1962)
AAA_Yield
Current Moody's AAA corporate bond yield, fetched live from FRED on each run

Version B (Graham_Graham_VB): EPS × (7 + Growth%) × 4.4 ÷ AAA_Yield

Version B uses a lower base P/E (7 vs. 8.5) and a single-multiple growth term instead of double — a more conservative variant of the same rate-adjusted formula.

Graham_Graham_FV = MIN(VA, VB) — always uses the more conservative of the two estimates as the definitive fair value.

Graham Discount

Column: Graham_Graham_Discount_Pct — measures how far the current price is below Graham fair value. A positive value means the stock is trading at a discount (good — potential margin of safety). A negative value means it is trading at a premium to fair value.

Graham Defensive Investor Checklist

Graham defined criteria that a stock must meet to be suitable for a conservative "defensive" investor — one who prioritises safety of principal and a reasonable return. Each criterion earns one point; the total out of 8 is the defensive score.

Columns: DefensiveLabel (Pass / Borderline / Fail) and DefensiveScore (0–8).

Pass
Score ≥ 6 out of 8 — meets Graham's defensive investor standard
Borderline
Score 4–5 — partially meets the criteria; warrants closer review
Fail
Score < 4 — does not meet the defensive investor threshold

Eight-Point Checklist

# Criterion Pass Condition
1 Market capitalisation ≥ $2 billion
2 Current ratio ≥ 2.0 (current assets ÷ current liabilities)
3 Long-term debt / equity ≤ 1.0
4 Earnings per share history Positive EPS in 8 of the last 10 fiscal years
5 Dividend history Paid a dividend in 5 of the last 10 years
6 EPS growth (10-year) Cumulative growth ≥ 33% over 10 years (~3%/yr)
7 P/E ratio ≤ 15 based on 3-year average EPS
8 P/B ratio ≤ 1.5, or P/E × P/B ≤ 22.5

Data Sources

All data is fetched automatically on each screener run. No manual data entry is required. The screener runs on a weekday schedule via GitHub Actions.

Price, EPS, dividends, balance sheet
Yahoo Finance via the yfinance Python library
AAA corporate bond yield
FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) — fetched live on each run
Index constituents (S&P 500, Dow 30, Nasdaq-100)
Wikipedia — scraped on each run to keep the universe current

Growth is calculated as EPS CAGR using the longest available window (typically 4–5 years). If growth is negative it is floored at 1% so a conservative valuation is still computed.
